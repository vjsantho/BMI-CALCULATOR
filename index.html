BMI Introduction

BMI is a measurement of a person's leanness or corpulence based on their height and weight. It is widely used as a general indicator of whether a person has a healthy body weight for their height. It serves as a useful guide to determine if any additional health assessment is required.

Health Indicator: BMI provides a general idea of a person’s health status related to weight.

Risk Assessment: Helps identify potential health risks like heart disease, diabetes, and hypertension.

Fitness Tracking: Useful for individuals managing their weight or setting fitness goals.

BMI Formula

USC (Imperial) Units:

BMI = (703 × weight (lbs)) / (height (in) × height (in))

Example: BMI = (703 × 160) / (70 × 70) = 23.0

SI (Metric) Units:

BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m²)

Example: BMI = 72.57 / (1.778 × 1.778) = 23.0
